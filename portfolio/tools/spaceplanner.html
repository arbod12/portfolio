What SpacePlanner does

SpacePlanner is a to-scale floor planner. Everything you see is drawn at real-world size: one grid square is exactly one foot, and a 60-inch sofa takes up 60 inches of room. If it fits on screen, it fits in the room. You can work in a flat 2D plan or orbit the space in 3D, and the two views stay perfectly in sync.

It comes preloaded with a full 1158 sq ft two-bedroom apartment so you have something real to move around immediately. Reset any time from the Planner tab.

Every tool, and how to use it

◧ 2D / ◳ 3D toggle

  • 2D plan is the top-down floor plan. Best for precise placement, measuring, and editing doors. Furniture shows its footprint and label.
  • 3D view renders the rooms as real volumes with walls and furniture blocks. Drag empty space to orbit, scroll or pinch to zoom, and right-drag (or two-finger drag) to pan. Drag a furniture block to slide it across the floor.
  • ▣ Walls hides or shows the walls in 3D so you can see into the rooms from above.

View selector

  • Choose Whole apartment to plan across every room at once, or pick a single room to zoom in and work on just that space. Doors, windows, and wall dimensions show automatically.

Moving & arranging furniture

  • Drag any piece to move it. It snaps to the inch and cannot leave the room it belongs to.
  • Tap a piece to select it. The readout shows its exact distance to all four walls. A piece turns red if it overlaps another.
  • ⟳ Rotate 90° spins it a quarter turn; ⟲ 15° and ⟳ 15° nudge the angle for diagonal placement.
  • ⧉ Duplicate drops an identical copy next to it; 🗑 Delete removes it.

Adding furniture

  • Type a label, its width, depth, and height in inches, pick a color, then ＋ Add furniture. It appears in the room you are viewing and you drag it into place. Sizes are exact, so measure your real furniture and enter those numbers.

📏 Measure

  • Turn it on, then press and drag between any two points. The tape reads the straight-line distance in inches and feet, plus the horizontal and vertical span. Clear tape removes it.

Rooms

  • Add a room by entering a name, width, length, and ceiling height, then ＋ Add. New rooms appear to scale and you can place furniture in them.
  • Delete current room removes the room you are currently viewing and its furniture.

Doors, walkways & windows

  • Pick a wall (N/E/S/W), a type, a hinge side for doors, then a position along the wall and a width. ＋ Add places it. Doors cut a real gap and draw their swing; walkways are open gaps; windows sit in the wall.
  • Select an existing opening from the dropdown to edit or delete it. Wall dimension labels recalculate automatically.

Reset

  • Reset to imported apartment restores the original floor plan, furniture, and openings. Layouts live in this browser session only, so reset is also your undo-all.

💾 Save & 📂 open project files

  • Save .splan downloads your entire project (every room, piece, door, and setting) as a small .splan file. This is the true save: it keeps all the editable data.
  • Open .splan loads a previously saved file straight back into the planner so you can keep editing and re-export. Because layouts reset on refresh, the .splan file is how you carry work between sessions or share it.

Exporting drawings (PNG · JPEG · SVG · PDF)

  • PNG — high-resolution image with transparent-free white background, great for dropping into docs or email.
  • JPEG — same drawing, smaller file, solid white background.
  • SVG — vector format that stays razor-sharp at any size and opens in design tools (Illustrator, Figma, Inkscape) for further editing.
  • PDF — a print-ready, letter-size sheet that auto-fits the drawing. With Include title block & scale ticked, it adds a professional title block (plan name, area, overall dimensions, piece count, date) and a graphic 5-foot scale bar.
  • Exports follow the current view: the whole apartment, or a single room if you have one selected. In 3D mode, PNG/JPEG/PDF capture the rendered 3D view instead of the flat plan.

📋 Furniture schedule (CSV)

  • Exports an architectural takeoff: every piece with its width, depth, height, footprint in square feet, and rotation, followed by a room-by-room area summary and the total. Opens in Excel or Google Sheets for spec sheets, ordering, or budgeting.

Notes on accuracy

The preloaded apartment is calibrated to its labeled room dimensions: Primary Bedroom 11'2"×13'2", Living/Dining 12'5"×16'4", and Bedroom 2 10'10"×11'10". Other rooms were measured against those references from the floor plan. Furniture uses standard real-world sizes; replace any piece with your own exact measurements for a true fit check. Everything renders client-side and nothing is uploaded.
